Prediction Accuracy: ClimateAP VS. WorldClim

To objectively assess the predictive accuracy of ClimateAP against WorldClim data, this study uses observed climate data from weather stations as a benchmark. Regressing these observed values on the predictions from ClimateAP and WorldClim allows for a direct comparison of accuracy. By examining the results from both sets of regressions, we can determine which climate data source more accurately reflects real-world conditions.

Methodology

To assess the precision of ClimateAP's climatic predictions, we applied Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) regression, which can be represented as:

$$ y = X\beta + \epsilon $$

where:

  • \( y \) is the vector of observed climate values,
  • \( X \) is the matrix containing a column of ones and ClimateAP's predictions,
  • \( \beta \) represents the regression coefficients,
  • \( \epsilon \) denotes the residuals.

The matrices are detailed as follows:

$$ y = \begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\\\ y_2 \\\\ \vdots \\\\ y_n \end{bmatrix},\quad X = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & x_1 \\\\ 1 & x_2 \\\\ \vdots & \vdots \\\\ 1 & x_n \end{bmatrix},\quad \beta = \begin{bmatrix} \beta_0 \\\\ \beta_1 \end{bmatrix},\quad \epsilon = \begin{bmatrix} \epsilon_1 \\\\ \epsilon_2 \\\\ \vdots \\\\ \epsilon_n \end{bmatrix} $$

Performance metrics such as RMSE and R2 are computed to evaluate the model's predictive accuracy and the explanatory power:

RMSE: $$ RMSE = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2} $$

R2: $$ R^2 = 1 - \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \hat{y}_i)^2}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - \bar{y})^2} $$

Adjusted R2: $$ adj. R^2 = 1 - \left(\frac{(1 - R^2)(n - 1)}{n - p - 1}\right) $$
